Our Story

About Welsh Dragon Rugby

Built by a lifelong Welsh rugby supporter — for Welsh rugby supporters everywhere.

The Person Behind the Site

My name is John James. I have been following Welsh rugby for as long as I can remember — and that takes me back to around 1965.

Over sixty years of watching Wales. Six decades of Six Nations Saturdays, regional rugby, club rugby, women's rugby, Under-20s, cup runs, heartbreaks, Grand Slams and dark periods. I have lived through all of it — and I would not swap a single moment of it.

My club is the Ospreys. Always has been. South Wales rugby runs through me, and the Ospreys represent everything I love about regional rugby — the passion, the identity, the connection to the community. I follow them through every season, good and bad.

But Welsh rugby for me goes far beyond the Ospreys. I follow the Welsh national team obsessively. I watch the Wales Women's team with immense pride — they deserve far more recognition than they get. And I follow the Wales Under-20s closely, because that is where tomorrow's heroes are being built today.

Welsh Dragon Rugby is this site. Built from that passion. Written from that experience. No corporate agenda. No scraped content. Just a genuine Welsh rugby fan who wanted a proper home for the sport he loves.

Welsh rugby is not just a sport to me.
It is part of who I am — part of where I come from.

Since 1965, the red jersey has meant everything.
It still does.

What This Site Is

Welsh Dragon Rugby is an independent fan site covering Welsh rugby at every level. Live scores, fixtures, standings and original opinion articles — all in one place, built for supporters who care as deeply about the game as I do.

We are not affiliated with the WRU or any regional club. We have no press access, no official relationship, no commercial arrangement with any team. We are simply supporters covering the sport we love.

Every article on this site is written with genuine passion and honest opinion. If something is not working in Welsh rugby, we will say so. If something gives us real reason for hope, we will say that too.
